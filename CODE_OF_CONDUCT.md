# Code of Conduct

## Expected behavior

Be respectful, constructive, and security-conscious when participating in this
project.

This applies to issues, pull requests, discussions, reviews, and other
project-managed communication channels.

## Unacceptable behavior

The following behavior is not acceptable:

- harassment, discrimination, threats, or personal attacks;
- disruptive or intentionally misleading participation;
- publishing credentials, cookies, tokens, webhook URLs, private account
  information, or other secrets;
- pressuring contributors or maintainers to disclose private information,
  credentials, security-sensitive implementation details, or unsafe
  reproduction data;
- encouraging bypasses of access controls, CAPTCHA, account-security
  mechanisms, or service restrictions.

## Security-sensitive content

Do not publish suspected vulnerabilities or exposed secrets in a public issue.

Follow the private reporting instructions in
[SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md). If a secret is posted publicly, project maintainers
may edit, hide, lock, or delete the affected content to reduce exposure.

Exposed credentials should be revoked or rotated immediately. Removing a post
does not invalidate a leaked secret.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may edit or remove content, close or lock discussions,
reject contributions, and temporarily or permanently restrict participation
when necessary to protect participants and the project.

Enforcement decisions may consider the severity, impact, intent, repetition,
and willingness to correct the behavior.
